## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes requirements for protecting Protected Health Information (PHI) in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

## 2. SCOPE

Applies to all:
- Employees
- Contractors
- Business associates
- Any party with access to PHI

---

## 3. DEFINITIONS

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| PHI | Protected Health Information - Individually identifiable health information |
| ePHI | Electronic Protected Health Information |
| Covered Entity | Health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, healthcare provider |
| Business Associate | Person/entity that performs functions involving PHI |
| Minimum Necessary | Use/disclose only minimum PHI needed |

---

## 4. PHI CATEGORIES

### 4.1 Types of PHI

- Patient names
- Dates (birth, admission, discharge)
- Phone numbers, addresses
- Social Security numbers
- Medical record numbers
- Health plan numbers
- Biometric identifiers
- Photos
- Any info that identifies health condition

### 4.2 High-Risk PHI

- Mental health records
- HIV/AIDS status
- Substance abuse records
- Genetic information

## 6. PHYSICAL SAFEGUARDS

### 6.1 Facility Access

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|--------------|
| Badge access | Required for PHI areas |
| Visitor log | Sign in/out |
| Escort required | For unbadged visitors |
| Video surveillance | In PHI areas |

### 6.2 Workstation Security

- [ ] Position screens away from public view
- [ ] Use privacy screens
- [ ] Lock when unattended
- [ ] Clear screens between patients

### 6.3 Device Controls

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Encryption | Full disk |
| Passwords | Complex, 8+ chars |
| MFA | Required for ePHI access |
| Remote wipe | Enabled for mobile |

---

## 7. TECHNICAL SAFEGUARDS

### 7.1 Access Control

| Control | Implementation |
|---------|----------------|
| Unique user ID | Required for all users |
| Automatic logoff | 15 minutes |
| Emergency access | Documented procedure |
| Access auditing | Log all access |

### 7.2 Encryption

| Data State | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| At rest | AES-256 or equivalent |
| In transit | TLS 1.2+ |
| Email | TLS or encrypted |

### 7.3 Audit Controls

- [ ] Log all access to ePHI
- [ ] Review logs monthly
- [ ] Retain logs 6 years
- [ ] Protect log integrity

---

## 8. POLICIES AND PROCEDURES

### 8.1 Minimum Necessary

- Use/disclose only what's needed
- Limit access to job function
- Review access rights quarterly

### 8.2 Patient Rights

| Right | Response Time |
|-------|----------------|
| Access request | 30 days |
| Amendment request | 60 days |
| Accounting of disclosures | 60 days |
| Privacy complaint | Immediate |

### 8.3 Breach Notification

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Assess breach | 72 hours |
| Notify individuals | 60 days |
| Notify HHS | Annually or per incident |
| Media notice | If >500 affected |

## 9. BUSINESS ASSOCIATES

### 9.1 Requirements

All business associates must:
- Sign BAA before accessing PHI
- Implement safeguards
- Report breaches
- Return/destroy PHI on termination

## 10. INCIDENT RESPONSE

### 10.1 Suspected Breach

```
1. IMMEDIATE: Contain the breach
2. Within 1 hour: Notify Security Officer
3. Within 24 hours: Document incident
4. Within 72 hours: Risk assessment
5. As needed: Notify authorities
```

### 10.2 Risk Assessment

| Factor | Questions |
|--------|-----------|
| Nature of PHI | What was exposed? |
| Extent of breach | How many affected? |
| Unauthorized person | Who accessed? |
| Data protected | Was it encrypted? |
| Mitigation | What was done? |
